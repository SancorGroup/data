Providence Parcel: Strategic Roadmap

A Detailed Financial Analysis and Implementation Plan

Financial Analysis Deep Dive: Why Ownership is the Best Path

The analysis confirms that **Scenario 1**, direct acquisition and self-development, offers the most significant long-term financial benefits for L&W Supply. This approach is a classic **value-add development play** that maximizes control and asset appreciation, providing a solid, predictable return profile.

Compelling Returns

A **12% Internal Rate of Return (IRR)** and a **2.0x equity multiple** signify a doubling of equity over a 10-year horizon. This is achieved by combining a strong yield on cost with expected capital appreciation.

Risk Management

Financial risk is tied to **cost escalation**. The model shows that a 10% overrun still yields a respectable **10.8% IRR**, highlighting the project's resilience. Mitigating this with a **guaranteed maximum price (GMP) contract** is a key part of the strategy.

Strategic Control

Unlike a ground lease, ownership provides full control over a mission-critical asset, allowing L&W Supply to tailor the facility to its exact operational needs and capture all the long-term value created in a supply-constrained market.

IRR Sensitivity to Market Variables

The project's IRR is highly responsive to favorable rent growth and exit cap rate compression, while demonstrating measured resilience against construction cost escalation.

Strategic Roadmap: L&W Supply Direct Acquisition

This phased plan outlines the specific actions and timelines for L&W Supply to execute the direct acquisition and immediate development, ensuring a seamless transition and maximum value capture.

Phase 1: Immediate Action (0-3 Months)

  • Secure the land with a Purchase and Sale Agreement.
  • Engage project team (Architect, Engineer, General Contractor, Environmental Consultant).
  • Initiate talks with lenders to lock in **6% fixed-rate financing** at a **70% loan-to-cost**.

Phase 2: Entitlement & Permitting (3-9 Months)

  • Submit final plans for zoning and site plan review.
  • Obtain all necessary building permits.
  • Proactively engage with local officials to streamline the process.
  • Coordinate with utility providers for tie-ins.

Phase 3: Construction & Occupancy (9-20 Months)

  • Break ground and begin construction under a GMP contract.
  • Implement a project management system for progress tracking.
  • Complete final inspections for the certificate of occupancy.
  • Begin interior build-out and occupy the new facility.

Project Costs Breakdown

The majority of the budget is allocated to hard costs, with prudent allowances for soft costs and financing to ensure a smooth development process.
